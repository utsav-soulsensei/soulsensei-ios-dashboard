Positive signals
Metric What the data shows Scale / magnitude So what
Revenue
Daily run rate		 Apr daily average of ₹16.8k is the highest ever, marking steady monthly growth since October. → +81% vs Oct (₹9.3k/day)
↑ +13% vs Mar (₹14.8k/day)
Apr run rate = ₹5.1L/month		 Unlike iOS, Android revenue is experiencing consistent, month-over-month growth rather than choppy peaks and troughs. The core monetisation engine here is deeply stable.
Volume
Group sessions		 Group revenue dominates Android (73% share in Apr), completely replacing the winter dependency on 1:1 sessions (53% share in Feb). Feb 1:1 mix: 53.6%
Apr 1:1 mix: 26.8%
↑ Group dominance		 This is highly scalable. While iOS is reliant on expensive 1:1 capacity, Android users are scaling the core group features. Focus product efforts on group engagement for Android users.
Acquisition
New buyer %		 New buyers make up an incredibly stable 50-55% share month-over-month consistently. Oct: 50.6%
Jan: 55.4%
Apr: 50.8%		 The platform has found an equilibrium. It successfully acquires fresh cohorts while retaining exactly enough repeat buyers to build layered LTV. Maintain this 50/50 split natively.
Attribution
Dark traffic		 Only 28.0% of Android purchases are "undefined", compared to roughly ~67% on iOS. Tracking is working. Undefined: 28%
Tracked: 72%
↑ High visibility		 Because tracking is successful here, you can trust your channel mix on Android. Marketing capital deployed against Android campaigns can be measured and optimized successfully.
Owned channels
Notifications		 Push notifications drive a highly impactful 16.2% of all attributed traffic (double the impact of iOS). 16.2% share of all purchases
Zero paid spend		 Android users natively engage with system-level notifications more readily. This is a massively valuable wedge: lean extensively into targeted, zero-cost push messages.
Negative signals
Metric What the data shows Scale / magnitude So what
ROAS
Collapse in efficiency		 ROAS collapsed structurally from legendary levels (4.24x in Nov) directly down to functional unprofitability entirely (1.07x in Apr). Nov avg: 4.24x
Jan avg: 3.55x
Apr avg: 1.07x
↓ −74% deterioration		 Unlike iOS, which bounced back slightly, Android ROAS is in freefall over six consecutive months. What broke here? The creatives are suffering intense fatigue on Android channels specifically. Budget must be trimmed hard.
Conversion
Low baseline		 Average CR is currently 1.50% (April), remaining suppressed beneath October's 2.09% high. It remains stubbornly lower than iOS. Oct peak: 2.09%
Apr avg: 1.50%
iOS Apr: 2.16%
↓ −28% vs baseline		 For every 100 Android users acquired, you are converting 0.5 fewer natively, and ~0.6 fewer than iOS users today. The Android flow requires serious UX/performance auditing quickly.
Volume
Purchase cadence		 Despite record revenue, purchase volume in April is flatly averaging 10.7 purchases/day (vs 11-13 earlier in the year). Ticket size is compensating for lower counts. Jan count: 11.2/day
Apr count: 10.7/day
↓ Volume softening		 Revenue growth driven exclusively by extracting higher ACV from an equal/smaller volume of buyers eventually hits a ceiling. Volume growth is required to compound.
Watch carefully
Metric What the data shows Scale / magnitude So what
Decoupling
Platform divergence		 Android and iOS metrics are moving completely opposite to each other. iOS is scaling 1:1, sinking in Group; Android is scaling Group, sinking in 1:1. iOS: 57% 1:1 mix
Android: 26% 1:1 mix		 Your platform essentially operates two different businesses right now. The same global go-to-market plan will not work simultaneously for a high-ticket 1:1 iOS base, and a low ticket Group Android base.
Paid vs Organic
Scaling dependency		 With ROAS entering loss-making territory (< 1.5x) but overall revenue increasing, Android may be leaning heavily on organic brand searches rather than direct response. ROAS falling
Revenue rising		 If paid completely turns off (due to poor ROAS), you need to know exactly how much of the ₹16.8k/day will organically survive. Test lower spend thresholds immediately.

Metric definitions
MetricDefinitionUnit / source
Conversion Rate Share of app page-views that result in a completed purchase. Averaged within each time bucket. % · CR_RAW
ROAS Return on Ad Spend — revenue generated per ₹1 of paid ad spend. Outliers above 20× excluded. × · ROAS_RAW
New buyer % First-time buyers as a share of total purchases in the bucket. High = healthy top-of-funnel. % · NR_RAW
1:1 rev share 1:1 (personal) session revenue as a % of total revenue (group + 1:1). Tracks product-mix shift. % · REV_RAW
Total purchases Raw count of all purchases (new + repeat) in the bucket. Proxy for overall demand volume. count · NR_RAW
Total revenue Sum of group + 1:1 session revenue in the bucket. Absolute ₹ value before normalisation. ₹ · REV_RAW
Direct % Self-initiated / direct purchases as a % of all attributed purchases. Organic intent signal. % · SRC_RAW
Paid % Purchases attributed to paid ad channels as a % of all attributed purchases. % · SRC_RAW
