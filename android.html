Deep-dive findings (funnel · releases · LTV · retention)
Finding What the data shows Scale / magnitude So what
Funnel
May OTP collapse		 Android Paid PDP funnel May 2026: PDP views nearly doubled (2,601 → 4,571), but OTP → Login fell from 84% to 20%, and Login → Purchase from 75% to 25%. Overall paid CR collapsed from 2.07% to 0.74%. Apr Paid CR: 2.07%
May Paid CR: 0.74%
↓ −64% in one month
PDP views: +76% MoM		 Either the OTP delivery service degraded, or paid campaigns flooded the top of the funnel with low-intent / bot traffic. Same-day triage on SMS delivery rates and a campaign-source audit would isolate which. ROAS is propped up at ~1× only by the volume spike.
Funnel
Auth is the biggest leak		 On the Paid Course PDP, logged-in users convert at ~2× the rate of logged-out users — OTP + Login eat roughly half the conversion in normal months. Logged-in CR avg: ~5.0%
Logged-out CR avg: ~2.7%
↑ ~2×		 Pre-authenticating more visitors is the single biggest CR lever. Anything that keeps users logged in (silent re-auth, biometric quick-login, persistent sessions) returns asymmetric impact vs. funnel UI tweaks.
Release impact
JWT + login flow		 v0.7.8 (Apr 28) "JWT token expire fixed · subscription · FB deferred deeplink" should have improved login-state retention — yet Android CR continues to slip. The fix may be cancelled out by the May paid-traffic quality crash. v0.7.8 Apr 28
Android May CR: 1.07% (lowest in dataset)		 The auth-side improvement isn't visible in the metrics because traffic quality dominates. Worth re-validating the JWT fix in isolation on a clean traffic source before assuming it works.
LTV
Cohort dilution — severe		 Per-user LTV has collapsed as cohort size grew: Jun 2025 ₹9,078 (15 users) → Apr 2026 ₹1,802 (92 users, the largest cohort). Peak: ₹9,078 (Jun 2025)
Latest: ₹1,802 (Apr 2026)
↓ −80% peak → latest		 Android is buying many more users at much lower per-user value. Apr 2026 is the worst combination in the dataset — biggest cohort × lowest LTV. Paid spend efficiency has degraded by a multiple, even though ROAS reads at ~1× (which is itself near break-even).
Retention
New-buyer share dropping		 May 2026 new-buyer share fell to 39% — the lowest in the dataset, down from 50% in April. Combined with falling CR and falling LTV, this is the third symptom of paid-traffic quality erosion. Jan high: 55%
Apr: 50%
May: 39%
↓ −11 pp Apr→May		 Revenue is increasingly carried by existing buyers. Without a new-acquisition rebound, total growth will cap at the existing base's repeat behaviour — currently a modest M01 user-repeat in the 7–17% range.
Release impact
v0.5.0 listing redesign		 v0.5.0 (Jan 8) "Listing page new design · bottom nav redesign · 10-min attendance" preceded a sustained CR uptick (Feb peaked at 1.97% before later collapse). Pre-v0.5.0 CR: ~1.2%
Post-v0.5.0 peak (Feb): 1.97%
↑ +0.8 pp short-term		 The gain proved temporary — by May, CR was 1.07%. Either the redesign benefit decayed, or it was masked by the same paid-quality issue affecting May broadly. A controlled re-introduction of the design on a clean cohort would isolate which.
Funnel
HP recovery		 Android HP → Listing rate climbed from a Dec low of 13% to 42% by May 2026 — its highest ever. Dec 2025: 13.3%
May 2026: 42.4%
↑ +29 pp		 The top of the Android funnel is structurally healthier than ever — the issue is no longer "users finding the catalog". Investment should shift entirely to deeper-funnel fixes (PDP → Cart → Payment), where the May collapse occurred.

Revenue
Best month ever		 Apr closed at ₹5.6L total revenue — the highest in the dataset. May (21 days) is tracking ₹17.8k/day, on pace for ~₹5.5L. Oct: ₹9.0k/day
Mar: ₹14.8k/day
Apr: ₹18.6k/day
May run rate: ₹17.8k/day
↑ +106% vs Oct		 Android revenue continues its steady multi-month upward trend. Apr was a clear step-up, and May is holding that new level. The monetisation engine here is structurally compounding.
Volume
Group sessions		 Group revenue dominates Android (68% share in Apr, 61% in May). Stays a fundamentally different mix vs iOS's 1:1-led model. Feb 1:1 mix: 54%
Apr 1:1 mix: 32%
May 1:1 mix: 39%
↑ Group-led		 Highly scalable: Android users are scaling the core group features rather than capacity-constrained 1:1. Continue investing product effort in group engagement for this audience.
Volume
Apr purchases		 Apr hit 348 total purchases — comfortably the highest month, +35% vs Mar (247). Mar: 247 purchases
Apr: 348 purchases
May 21d: 224
↑ +35% Mar→Apr		 Unlike iOS where Apr's revenue was lifted by 1:1 ticket size, Android grew by selling more units. That's the healthier driver and easier to sustain if the channel mix holds.
Attribution
Dark traffic		 Only ~47% of Android purchases are "undefined", vs ~77% on iOS. Tracking is meaningfully better. Undefined: ~47%
Tracked: ~53%
↑ vs iOS		 Channel attribution on Android is reliable enough to inform spend decisions. iOS still needs a UTM hygiene pass to catch up.
Owned channels
Notifications		 Notification + push_notification together drive ~17% of attributed Android purchases at zero cost. ~17% share of attributed purchases
Zero paid spend		 Android users engage with system-level notifications far more readily than iOS. A high-leverage, zero-cost channel — keep investing in segmentation and copy quality.

ROAS
Stuck near 1x		 Android ROAS has collapsed from Nov's 4.24x peak and is now stuck around 1x — May 1.08x continues the trend. Nov avg: 4.24x
Jan avg: 3.55x
Apr: 1.03x
May: 1.08x
↓ −75% vs peak		 Six consecutive months of degradation and no recovery in sight. Unlike iOS which has bounced toward 1.5x, Android paid is effectively break-even. Creatives are fatigued — budget should be trimmed hard until a fresh test cycle proves above 2x.
Conversion
Slipping further		 Android CR fell to 1.07% in May — the lowest since Dec. Apr (1.47%) and May together sit well below Oct's 2.09% high. Oct peak: 2.09%
Apr: 1.47%
May: 1.07%
iOS May: 2.06%
↓ −49% May vs Oct		 The funnel is getting worse, not healing. May Android CR is half of iOS — for every 100 visitors, ~1 fewer buys than on iOS. Serious UX/perf audit warranted on the Android conversion flow.
New buyer %
Sharp May drop		 New buyer share fell from 50% (Apr) to 39% (May) — the lowest in the dataset by a wide margin. Jan high: 55%
Apr: 50%
May: 39%
↓ −11pp Apr→May		 If this holds, Android is now monetising existing buyers rather than acquiring new ones. Combined with the lower CR, this signals top-of-funnel is choking. New-acquisition campaigns need urgent attention.
Volume
May softening		 May (21 days) is pacing 10.7 purchases/day vs Apr's 11.6 — a slight cooldown after Apr's strong run. Apr: 11.6/day (348 total)
May 21d: 10.7/day (224 total)
May run rate: ~330
↓ Softer		 Apr's volume surge isn't holding fully. Combined with falling CR and new-buyer share, the volume engine needs careful watching — Apr may have been a peak rather than a new floor.
Watch carefully

Decoupling
Platform divergence		 iOS and Android continue to diverge. iOS leans 1:1 (45–56%) with rebounding CR/ROAS; Android is group-led (61–68%) with falling CR and new-buyer share. iOS Apr/May 1:1: 56%/45%
Android Apr/May 1:1: 32%/39%
iOS May CR: 2.06%
Android May CR: 1.07%		 Two structurally different businesses. The same global go-to-market plan can't work for both — iOS needs premium/1:1 packaging, Android needs funnel + group-engagement fixes.
Paid vs Organic
Scaling dependency		 Android ROAS is stuck near break-even (1.0–1.1x) while revenue grows. The growth is likely organic, not paid-driven. ROAS flat at ~1.0x
Revenue up +20% Mar→Apr
May holding Apr levels		 If paid is cut due to poor ROAS, you need to know exactly how much of the ₹17.8k/day will organically survive. Test stepped spend reductions now to find the organic baseline.
May funnel quality
Watch CR + new buyer		 CR (1.07%) and new-buyer share (39%) both hit dataset lows in May. Revenue is held up by ticket size, not flow health. May CR: 1.07% (low)
May new buyer: 39% (low)
May rev: ₹3.7L (21d)		 If May was a one-off, June will rebound. If not, you're seeing a structural decline in funnel quality masked by stronger ticket sizes. June data will be decisive.

Metric definitions
MetricDefinitionUnit / source
Conversion Rate Share of app page-views that result in a completed purchase. Averaged within each time bucket. % · CR_RAW
ROAS Return on Ad Spend — revenue generated per ₹1 of paid ad spend. Outliers above 20× excluded. × · ROAS_RAW
New buyer % First-time buyers as a share of total purchases in the bucket. High = healthy top-of-funnel. % · NR_RAW
1:1 rev share 1:1 (personal) session revenue as a % of total revenue (group + 1:1). Tracks product-mix shift. % · REV_RAW
Total purchases Raw count of all purchases (new + repeat) in the bucket. Proxy for overall demand volume. count · NR_RAW
Total revenue Sum of group + 1:1 session revenue in the bucket. Absolute ₹ value before normalisation. ₹ · REV_RAW
Direct % Self-initiated / direct purchases as a % of all attributed purchases. Organic intent signal. % · SRC_RAW
Paid % Purchases attributed to paid ad channels as a % of all attributed purchases. % · SRC_RAW
