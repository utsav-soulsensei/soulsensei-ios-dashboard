Deep-dive findings (funnel · releases · LTV · retention)
Finding What the data shows Scale / magnitude So what
Funnel
Auth is the biggest leak		 On the Paid Course PDP, logged-in users convert at ~2× the rate of logged-out users — the OTP + Login steps are the single largest deficit in the funnel. Logged-in PDP→Purchase: ~5.1% avg (last 6 mo)
Logged-out PDP→Purchase: ~2.6% avg
↑ ~2× the conversion rate		 Anything that pre-authenticates more visitors (silent login, persistent sessions, social login, biometric quick-auth) is the highest-leverage CR lever available. Reducing the logged-out share by even 20 pp could lift overall paid CR by ~20%.
Release impact
Apr ROAS recovery		 The Apr→May ROAS recovery (0.94× → 1.21× → 1.52×) maps tightly to v10 (Apr 24, ATT removed → temporary disruption), v10.1 (Apr 27, JWT refresh fix → fewer silent logouts), and v10.2 (Apr 28, ATT rolled back). Mar avg: 0.94×
Apr avg: 1.21×
May avg: 1.52×
v10.1 + v10.2 are likely causal		 The recovery is partly attribution-fix, not market improvement. The JWT fix likely shifted users into the higher-CR logged-in path. Don't assume the trend is purely organic — it may flatten when those one-time gains complete.
Release impact
Listing redesign		 v8.4 (Jan 7) "Listing pages redesign" lifted Listing → PDP rate from 52.3% (Jan) to 56.1% (Feb), and the gain has stuck (48–56% range since). Dec 2025: 53.0%
Feb 2026: 56.1%
↑ +3 pp step-change		 This is the cleanest single-release win in the dataset. Worth post-morteming what specifically worked (card density? CTA placement?) and applying the pattern to other listing surfaces.
Tracking caveat
May 15 event change		 v10.8 (May 15) changed the placement of the proceed_checkout event. May 1–14 and May 15–21 Paid funnel step rates measure subtly different things. v10.8 affects late-May data
"Proceed Payment → Payment" step is the most affected		 When judging late-May paid funnel performance, discount step-rate changes that align with v10.8 — they're likely measurement artefacts, not behaviour shifts. Re-baseline once a full post-v10.8 month is available (June).
LTV
Cohort dilution		 Per-user LTV is inversely correlated with cohort size: small early cohorts (May 2025: 15 users, ₹6,786 LTV) far outpace large recent ones (Nov 2025: 72 users, ₹3,390 / Apr 2026: 66 users, ₹2,464). May 2025 LTV: ₹6,786
Nov 2025 LTV: ₹3,390
Apr 2026 LTV: ₹2,464
↓ −64% peak → latest		 Scaling acquisition is bringing in materially lower-value users — paid spend is buying volume at the cost of quality. Until LTV stabilises, treat new-user growth as a vanity metric and watch payback per channel, not headline acquired-user count.
Retention
M01 plateau		 Across iOS cohorts, M01 user-repeat sits in a narrow 5–27% band with no clear trend over the last 6 cohorts. Revenue-repeat at M01 is similarly flat (~10–48%, but volatile due to small cohort sizes). Avg M01 user-repeat: ~16%
Avg M01 rev-repeat: ~21%
No upward trajectory in recent cohorts		 Retention isn't degrading, but it isn't improving either — the platform isn't getting "stickier" with newer cohorts. Until M01 user-repeat moves above 25% sustainably, growth has to come from new acquisition, not LTV expansion.
Funnel
HP recovery		 iOS HP → Listing rate climbed from ~30% in mid-2025 to ~38% by May 2026. v9.3 (Mar 2, new Library page + event renames) is the apparent inflection. Mid-2025: ~30%
May 2026: 38%
↑ +8 pp		 The top of the funnel is healthier than ever. Leakage is now concentrated deep in the auth + payment flow, not in browsing. Investment focus should shift from "discoverability" fixes to "checkout completion" fixes.

Best month ever		 Apr closed at ₹8.2L total — the highest monthly revenue in the dataset, beating Dec/Jan peaks of ₹7.1L Apr: ₹8.2L (₹27k/day)
Dec/Jan peak: ₹7.1L
↑ +15% vs prior best		 Apr decisively cleared the prior ceiling. Daily revenue is now sustained well above earlier peaks. The platform has structurally repriced upward.

May continuation		 May (21 days) is tracking ₹28.6k/day — slightly above Apr's pace, projecting to ~₹8.9L for the month Apr: ₹27k/day
May 21d avg: ₹28.6k/day
May run rate: ₹8.9L		 The Apr surge was not a one-off. May is sustaining (and marginally extending) the new daily revenue ceiling. This is the first back-to-back month of structurally elevated revenue.

1:1 sessions		 Apr 1:1 revenue hit ₹4.55L (56% of mix). May continues with ₹2.71L in 21 days at 45% share. Oct: 36% of mix

↑ +231% Apr 1:1 vs Oct		 1:1 is now structurally a much larger slice of revenue than at launch. May's softer 1:1 share suggests group sessions are pulling more weight again — the healthiest balance the platform has had.
Conversion
CR holding		 Apr (2.05%) and May (2.06%) CR are essentially tied — fully recovered from the Feb/Dec trough of 1.46% Feb/Dec low: 1.46%
Apr: 2.05%
May: 2.06%
↑ +41% vs trough		 Two consecutive months at ~2% indicates the conversion recovery is durable, not a one-month bounce. Still below Oct's 2.66% baseline but the funnel is clearly healthier.
Owned channels
Notifications		 Push + notification + push_notification together contribute ~7% of attributed purchases at near-zero cost ~7% share of all attributed purchases
Zero paid spend		 This is your most efficient marketing channel by a wide margin. Every ₹ invested in improving push notification quality and segmentation has asymmetric return vs paid ads.
ROAS
Recovery signal		 May ROAS (1.52x avg) extends Apr's recovery (1.21x) from Mar's 0.94x bottom. Direction is clearly positive. Mar avg: 0.94x (loss)
Apr avg: 1.21x
May avg: 1.52x
↑ +62% vs trough		 Paid is no longer loss-making on average, and the trend is improving month-over-month. Still below Oct–Nov's 2.6x range; continue cautious scaling only above 2x.

Slipping		 May new buyer share dropped to 43% — back to the Feb trough level after April's 45% Best month: Nov 54%
Feb low: 43%
Apr: 45%
May: 43%
↓ −11pp vs Nov		 Revenue is increasingly carried by existing buyers. LTV monetisation is fine in the short run, but a falling new-buyer share signals weaker top-of-funnel — fix before it caps growth.
Conversion
CR vs Oct		 Oct CR averaged 2.66% — the dataset high. Apr/May plateau at 2.05% — still ~23% below baseline. Oct: 2.66%
Apr: 2.05%
May: 2.06%
↓ −23% vs baseline		 For every 100 visitors today, ~0.6 fewer convert than in October. Finding what drove Oct's high CR (specific campaign + landing combination) is still the highest-leverage lever.
Attribution
Dark traffic		 ~77% of all iOS purchases show as "undefined" / direct source — unattributed across all 8 months consistently ~77% of purchases with no source data
Consistent every month		 You cannot optimise what you cannot measure. This blind spot means marketing budget allocation decisions are effectively guesswork. UTM tagging on all practitioner shares, WhatsApp messages, and CRM links would immediately improve visibility.
Group sessions
Below peak		 Apr group revenue was ₹3.62L vs Dec peak of ₹4.74L. May is tracking ~₹4.7L run-rate, only just matching Dec. Dec: ₹4.74L group
Apr: ₹3.62L
May run rate: ~₹4.7L
↓ Still ≤ Dec peak		 The scalable revenue engine has not yet broken its Dec ceiling. Total revenue is at all-time highs only because 1:1 absorbed the gap. Until group rev breaks above ₹5L/month, the model is structurally capacity-constrained.

May volatility
Lumpy daily revenue		 May's ₹28.6k/day average hides high day-to-day variance — peaks of ₹54k+ (May 4, 18) interspersed with sub-₹8k days (May 17, 21). May max day: ~₹59k
May min day: ~₹7.5k
Avg: ₹28.6k/day		 The monthly average is healthy but the daily curve is volatile. A single quiet week could change the picture quickly. Worth understanding what drove the strong days.
Repeat buyers
Growing share		 Repeat buyers grew from 46% (Nov) to ~57% in Feb and remain at 55%+ in Apr–May. Nov: 46% repeat
Feb peak: 57%
Apr: 55%
May: 57%		 A healthy repeat base is good for LTV, but the slipping new-buyer share means the platform is increasingly monetising existing users rather than growing. New acquisition needs attention.
Instagram
Low conversion		 Instagram is a spiritual wellness platform's natural home, yet organic instagram + ig + Instagram tags contribute only ~1.5% of attributed iOS purchases ~1.5% of attributed purchases
Consistent and low across all months		 Either Instagram traffic isn't being captured (UTM gaps) or the Instagram → app conversion journey is broken. Worth auditing the link-in-bio funnel specifically.

Metric definitions
MetricDefinitionUnit / source
Conversion Rate Share of app page-views that result in a completed purchase. Averaged within each time bucket. % · CR_RAW
ROAS Return on Ad Spend — revenue generated per ₹1 of paid ad spend. Outliers above 20× excluded. × · ROAS_RAW
New buyer % First-time buyers as a share of total purchases in the bucket. High = healthy top-of-funnel. % · NR_RAW
1:1 rev share 1:1 (personal) session revenue as a % of total revenue (group + 1:1). Tracks product-mix shift. % · REV_RAW
Total purchases Raw count of all purchases (new + repeat) in the bucket. Proxy for overall demand volume. count · NR_RAW
Total revenue Sum of group + 1:1 session revenue in the bucket. Absolute ₹ value before normalisation. ₹ · REV_RAW
Direct % Self-initiated / direct purchases as a % of all attributed purchases. Organic intent signal. % · SRC_RAW
Paid % Purchases attributed to paid ad channels as a % of all attributed purchases. % · SRC_RAW
